# Domain Research - Slot machine functions

## Terminology
Illusion of control 
- Cognitive bias where individuals overestimate their influence over random events

Gambler’s fallacy 
- Incorrect belief that an event that has occurred less than expected is more likely to occur in the future. Ex: when coinflipping, after a streak of 6 heads, the next toss should be a tails.
  
Progression effect 
- Psychological satisfaction linked to the act of completing a sequence

---

## Features
Stopping device 
- Has been found to increase the sense of control, causing them to play more often than those without

Descending win pattern 
- By concentrating more wins towards the start, users feel more in control and also feel they win more. Also induces gamblers fallacy at the end from loses. This is opposed to ascending (more wins at end) and flat (neutral rates)

Daily rewards 
- Creates a habit / increases player retention. Also activates progression effect which gives the feeling of “accomplishing something”, fueling continuity.

Synchronized reels 
- Having 2 reels showing identical symbols make users feel like they have an increased chance of winning.

Social leaderboard 
- Creates competition / makes users feel more “skilled” / have something to show off

Leveling 
- Encourages users to hit next milestone / level before quitting

Bonus triggers / minigame 
- Makes users feel they have entered a “reward zone” 

---

## Player psychology
Illusion of control can cause increased betting / prolonged gambling sessions
Near misses trigger dopamine, encouraging more wins. Brain processes this loss as a “near win”
